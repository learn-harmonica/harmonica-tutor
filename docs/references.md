# Harmonica Tutor – References

This project links to publicly available learning resources and reference material. Embedded/linked media remains the property of its respective rights holders.

## History and instrument fundamentals

- **HOHNER – “Heritage”**
  - URL: https://hohner.de/en/company/heritage
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Mentions HOHNER founded by Matthias Hohner in Trossingen, Germany (1857) and the instrument’s role across Blues, Folk, Jazz, and Classical.

- **Encyclopaedia Britannica – “harmonica”**
  - URL: https://www.britannica.com/art/harmonica
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Basic construction, blow/draw concept, puckering vs tongue blocking, chromatic harmonica slide, historical note on Buschmann (c. 1821) and the sheng influence.

- **HOHNER – “The Best Professional Harmonica for Beginners”**
  - URL: https://hohner.de/en/community/the-best-professional-harmonica-for-beginners
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Recommends starting on key of C for beginners.

## Technique references (beginner to intermediate)

- **Harp Surgery – “Draw Bends”**
  - URL: https://www.harpsurgery.com/how-to-play/draw-bends/
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Conceptual explanation of bending mechanics (interaction between reeds sharing a chamber; relationship to pitch intervals).

## Prominent players and styles

- **Encyclopaedia Britannica – “Little Walter”**
  - URL: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Little-Walter
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Major figure in postwar Chicago blues; pioneer of playing harmonica directly into a handheld microphone; key recordings.

- **NPR – “Toots Thielemans, Jazz Harmonica Baron, Dies At 94”**
  - URL: https://www.npr.org/2016/08/22/490940271/toots-thielemans-jazz-harmonica-baron-has-died
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Bebop-influenced chromatic harmonica technique; “Bluesette” as a jazz standard.

- **NPR – “Harmonica Great DeFord Bailey Gets His Due”**
  - URL: https://www.npr.org/2005/11/12/5007645/harmonica-great-deford-bailey-gets-his-due
  - Accessed: 2025-12-24
  - Notes: Early Grand Ole Opry star; “Harmonica Wizard” framing and historical context.
